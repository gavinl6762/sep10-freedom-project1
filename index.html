Chess

Overview/Intro

  • Chess is a competitive board game that has been around for more than 100 years.
  • Many chess players would want to get better, but how?
  • I have found some chess apps and gadgets that could possibly be helpful.
Chess Clock App
  • Portable chess clock usable anywhere
  • Has delay and increment modes
  • Works on iOS, Android, and computers
Dr. Wolf
  • Chess learning coach app
  • Available on iOS and Android
  • Teaches step-by-step moves
Lichess
  • Free chess platform
  • Website and app available
  • Puzzles, tournaments, global players
  • Requires internet connection

Most Complex

Apps looked into: Dr. Wolf, Lichess, Chess Clock

  • These apps are useful when it comes to learning and improving chess especially with the use of chess engines and an AI coach, also can be used at anytime
  • The apps also helps you understand the game more by generating legal moves, evalutating positions, and searches through the amount of moves

Mildly Complex

Gadgets looked into: Chess clocks, sets, electronic chess boards

  • These gadgets are simple to use and could be helpful in some situations.
  • Can be used in friendly matches or tournaments
  • Not as portable because it's a real item that you'll have to bring

Least Complex

Apps looked into: Really Bad Chess, WorstFish

  • Portable, apps on the phone
  • For fun and entertainment, doesn't require any brainpower to play
  • No skill improvement, providing worst possible moves
