# Content
Topic: **Chess**

## Overview/Introduction/Context
* Chess is a competitive board game that has been around for more than 100 decades
* Many chess players would want to get better, but how?
* I have found some chess apps and gadgets that could possibly be helpful.

## Categories: 
* Most Complex
  * Chess apps
  * Apps looked into: Dr. Wolf, Lichess, Chess clock
  * These apps are useful for learning and improving chess especially with the use of chess engines and an AI coach, also can be used at anytime
  * The apps also helps you understand the game more by generating legal moves, evalutating positions, and searches through the amount of moves

* Mildly Complex
  * Chess gadgets
  * Gadgets looked into: Chess clocks, Traditional chess sets, Electronic chess boards
  * These gadgets are simple to use and could be helpful in some situations.
  * Can be used in friendly matches or tournaments
 
* Least Complex
  * Some chess apps
  * Apps looked into: Really Bad Chess, WorstFish
  * These apps doesn't require any sense of brainpower due to its formation.
  * Provides a joke engine that plays the worst moves possible, bugs that loses achievements, and a bad version of chess
